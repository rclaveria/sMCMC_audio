A sequential framework for music restoration


With the aim of addressing audio signal restoration as a sequential inference problem, we build upon batch Gabor regression models to construct a state-space model for audio time series. We exploit the probabilistic structure of the proposed model to derive exact Gibbs updates for the parameters within each state. Thus, sequential inference is carried out by means of sequential Markov Chain Monte Carlo (also known as MCMC-Particle filter), which is tested on a series of denoising examples. Results suggest that the sequential approach is competitive with batch strategies in terms of perceptual quality and signal-to-noise ratio, while showing potential for real-time applications---provided that enhanced inference strategies are developed.
